Your Professional Reputation is at Risk...

Google Chrome and Firefox (that's about 75% of market share) , declared open war to sites without SSL certificates, flagging them as dangerous places where your "credit cards and personal info can be stolen".

This is the year the internet as you know it, changes forever!

Google, along with other big players in the internet industry, are imposing new rules to the way companies promote, advertize, and brand their messages.

Right now, your site looks like this on Google Chrome.  An "i" inside a circle right next to your web address. If you click on it, you are informed that this site is not secure.

On Firefox instead of an "i" inside a circle, you get a padlock with a red line crossing it, indicating that your site is not safe.
Stay with us, because things are getting even worse...

This is what will eventually happen. Your site will be flagged as "dangerous", and people will be advised to go "Back to Safety"  because "Attackers might be trying to steal their passwords, messages, or credit cards".

This is when your reputation as a business is literally destroyed. 

And the few that ignore the previous message and manage to visit your site, they will see this: A constant reminder that they are not safe, as long as they stay at your business (that's the connection that happens inside the mind of the visitor).
