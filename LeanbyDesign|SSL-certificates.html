Your Professional Reputation is at Risk...

Google Chrome and Firefox (representing almost 75% of the market at the time this piece is written) , declared open war to sites without SSL certificates, flagging them as dangerous places where your "credit cards and personal info can be stolen".

This is the year the internet as you know it, changes forever!

And with it, so does your business reputation and good standing.

When this process is complete at some point in 2018, you will be left with only 2 options.

-Follow the new rules

or

-Be a "pariah". Stigmatized as an "unsafe" and "dangerous" business.

Keep reading to find out what's going on, and how to avoid being penalized.


Google started marking websites without SSL certificates as unsafe since the beginning of 2017. An SSL certificate, is that green lock you see next to a website you  visit. What it means, is that the site has security in place that prevents hackers or other bad parties from stealing your data. Data like:

-Your credit card details

-Your contact details

-Sensitive data you transmit through contact forms, like medical and legal records.

-Anything else that might put you in danger, or in trouble

The first "round" of this process ended at the beginning of January 2018.

This year.

What was the result of the first phase?

Check out the box below...

Right now, your site looks like this on Google Chrome.  An "i" inside a circle right next to your web address. If you click on it, you are informed that this site is not secure.

On Firefox instead of an "i" inside a circle, you get a padlock with a red line crossing it, indicating that your site is not safe.

Stay with us, because phase 2 just begun...

We are now on "phase 2". 

Google actually announced they were ready to deploy this plan since August 2017, but after pressure from other big companies and organizations, they agreed to postpone this for early 2018.

They also agreed to deploy the unsafe websites' flagging gradually.

(For all of you unfamiliar with Google practices, they inforce their changes immediately and without warning.)

This is giving you the time to prepare.

Time to make all necessary arrangements.

Buy the appropriate SSL certificates that suit your particular line of business, and avoid penalties.

(Yes, different companies need different certificates and levels of security. But we've got you covered, don't worry.)
