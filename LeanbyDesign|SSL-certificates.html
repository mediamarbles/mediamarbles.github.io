Google started marking websites without SSL certificates as unsafe since the beginning of 2017. An SSL certificate, is that green lock you see next to a website you visit. What it means, is that the site has security in place that prevents hackers or other bad parties from stealing your data.

Data like:

-Your credit card details

-Your contact details

-Sensitive data you transmit through contact forms, like medical and legal records.

-Anything else that might put you in danger, or in trouble

The first "round" of this process ended at the beginning of January 2018.
